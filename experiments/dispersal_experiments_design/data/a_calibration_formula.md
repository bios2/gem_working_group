# Calibration of the maximum dispersal rate

There are two ways to calibrate the maximum dispersal rates

### 1 - Using direct observation data
When we relate the maximum dispersal rate to mean observed dispersal distances found in the field.

Using the Ryser paper (2021), the maximum dispersal rate relates to the mean dispersal rates under neutral conditions (b = 0) with the following formula :

$$a = 2*disp_rate$$

The dispersal_rate can then be found using the diffusion formula :

$$disp_{rate} = \frac{D_{obs}^2}{\delta x^2*t}$$

Where $D_obs$ is the mean dispersal rate (km/generation) ; $\delta x²$ is the grid cell area and t is the generation time
